# Homepage Creative Brief + Visual Spec (Draft)

## Summary

Reframe the homepage so parents immediately understand Havi as a partner that helps them keep up with the pace and uncertainty of parenting, not just a tracker.

Core intent:
1. Vividly show the real parent problem (mental load + fragmented context).
2. Show Havi in action (conversation, memory, coordination, next steps).
3. Build trust with credible proof and customer voice.
4. Reduce repetition and increase narrative precision through a tighter messaging framework.

## Creative brief

### Audience

1. Busy parents and co-parents managing daily child context.
2. Families coordinating with helpers, relatives, and care partners.

### Problem to dramatize

Parents are not failing because they do not care. They are overloaded by fragmented context: many moments, many channels, many decisions, little time.

### Brand role

Havi is the partner in the parenting village that helps parents stay ahead and present by carrying context, surfacing insight, and coordinating action.

### Message promise

`Parenthood moves fast. Stay ahead and present with Havi.`

### Voice direction

1. Plain and warm.
2. Confident, not clinical.
3. Specific verbs over abstract claims.
4. Show behavior in product before stating outcomes.
5. Use fewer repeated claims; each section should add new information.

## Messaging + communication framework

Each narrative block should answer one distinct parent question.

1. `What is this?` (Hero)
   - `Parenthood moves fast. Stay ahead and present with Havi.`
2. `Why do I need this?` (Problem)
   - Mental load and fragmented context slow decisions.
3. `What changes with Havi?` (Without/With)
   - Concrete behavior shift, row by row.
4. `What can I actually do?` (4 pillars)
   - Daily product actions with concrete nouns.
5. `Why should I trust this?` (Proof + testimonials)
   - Expert-informed claim plus user outcomes.
6. `What do I do now?` (Close CTA)
   - Clear action toward signup.

## Narrative spec (show, not tell)

### Section 1: Hero (above fold)

Required copy:
1. Headline: `Parenthood moves fast. Stay ahead and present with Havi.`
2. Subhead (mechanism-first): `Track sleep, feeding, diapers, behavior, and routines in one shared thread. Talk with Havi like a partner to decide what to do next.`

Required visual:
1. One dominant product moment (zoomed, high clarity, no nested frame clutter).
2. Strong whitespace around hero copy and CTA.

### Section 2: Problem clarity (fast empathy)

Purpose:
1. Validate the lived experience in one short block.
2. Transition directly into transformation.

Copy pattern:
1. Problem statement line.
2. One sentence on cost of fragmented context.

### Section 3: Without / With comparison

Purpose:
1. Make transformation concrete.
2. Tie pain directly to product behavior.

Required structure:
1. Paired rows (`Without Havi` vs `With Havi`).
2. Each row should be one concrete behavior change.
3. Keep this section tight (4 rows max) to avoid repetition.

Approved copy rows (draft v1):
1. `Without Havi`: `Updates are scattered across texts and apps.`
   - `With Havi`: `Sleep, feeding, behavior, and notes stay in one thread.`
2. `Without Havi`: `Parents repeat the same context over and over.`
   - `With Havi`: `Havi remembers key details so everyone starts in sync.`
3. `Without Havi`: `Questions pile up when timing matters most.`
   - `With Havi`: `Ask in conversation and get next steps tailored to your child.`
4. `Without Havi`: `Plans drift between partners and helpers.`
   - `With Havi`: `Reminders, updates, and tasks stay aligned across your village.`

Visual spec:
1. Use one comparison card module, not multiple stacked card systems.
2. Keep supporting product graphic smaller for focus:
   - Desktop: comparison module occupies primary width; graphic uses ~32% to 40% of row width.
   - Mobile: place graphic after first two rows; keep height compact.
3. Avoid oversized decorative devices; prioritize legibility of row copy.

### Section 4: What parents do with Havi each day (4 pillars)

Heading:
1. `What parents do with Havi each day`

Pillars:
1. `Track everything in one place`
   - `Sleep, feeding, diapers, behavior, routines, and notes stay in one thread.`
2. `Get support through every phase`
   - `From sleepless nights to wild mornings, Havi stays with your family.`
3. `Get guidance tailored to your child`

4. `Keep your village in sync`
   - `Share updates, reminders, and plans so everyone knows what’s happening and what’s next.`

Show-not-tell rule:
1. Each pillar must be paired with a product evidence cue (screenshot crop, UI state, or concrete action line).

Optional expansion:

## Visual design spec

### Design goals

1. Premium clarity: less visual noise, more intentional hierarchy.
2. Better rhythm: clear breathing room between narrative beats.
3. Better brand presence: logo legibility and header hierarchy.

### Layout and whitespace

1. Use larger vertical spacing bands between major sections.
2. Keep section density low; avoid stacked heavy cards without canvas breaks.
3. On desktop, preserve readable line lengths and balanced two-column hero.
4. On mobile, preserve section order and keep headings short.
5. Reduce oversized graphics and cards; focus attention through whitespace rather than scale.

### Layering and surfaces

1. Reduce border nesting around product shots.
2. Use one dominant media frame plus subtle shadow.
3. Alternate deep and light canvas bands to avoid “all green” fatigue.

### Typography and brand clarity

1. Lock headline, section title, and body type roles.
2. Keep consistent heading weights by level.
3. Increase top-left brand lockup clarity (wordmark prominence and spacing).

## Product-positioning bridge

This homepage should frame Havi as both:
1. A daily memory and guidance partner for parenting questions and tracking.
2. A coordination layer that helps families actually get things done.

Do not present this as a timeline roadmap (“now/next” language on page).  
Do present it as one integrated job: capture, understand, coordinate, act.

## Acceptance criteria (binary)

1. Hero states both emotional outcome and concrete mechanism.
2. Four pillars are present, action-led, and mapped to product evidence.
3. Comparison module clearly expresses before/after behavior.
4. Trust line is visible before footer and not buried.
5. Visual rhythm includes clear light/deep contrast with intentional whitespace.
6. Header brand lockup is legible and visually prioritized.
7. If testimonials are shown, each quote includes specific outcome and attribution.
8. Comparison section uses exactly one tight module with four non-repetitive rows.
9. Supporting comparison graphic is intentionally smaller than the core message block.
